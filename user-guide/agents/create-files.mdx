## Files, not just replies

The Core Agent can produce **real files** you open, download, and share — documents, spreadsheet-style tables, and PDFs. You ask in plain English; the file shows up in the preview panel.

<Tip>
Say what you want **and** the format. *"…as a document I can download"* or *"…as a spreadsheet"* or *"…as a PDF"* steers the Agent clearly.
</Tip>

## Documents {#documents}

Use documents for briefs, summaries, proposals, emails drafts, meeting notes, and write-ups.

<Steps>
<Step title="Open Agents and start a session">
  Click **Agents**, then **+** for a new chat.
</Step>
<Step title="Describe the document">
  Include the purpose, length, and sections. Example:

  > *Write a one-page campaign brief with Goal, Audience, Key Message, and 3 Content Ideas. Make it a document I can download.*
</Step>
<Step title="Preview and download">
  Open the file on the right. Copy or download when you're happy.
</Step>
<Step title="Refine in the same chat">
  > *Add a Risks section and shorten the Key Message to one sentence.*
</Step>
</Steps>

**Good document asks**
- *"Turn these notes into a status update document for leadership."*
- *"Draft a simple SOW for a website redesign, as a downloadable doc."*
- *"Rewrite this email as a short internal announcement document."*

## Spreadsheets {#spreadsheets}

Use spreadsheets for lists, trackers, comparisons, and anything with rows and columns.

<Steps>
<Step title="Give the Agent structured material">
  Paste a list, CSV-ish text, or describe the columns you need.
</Step>
<Step title="Ask for a sheet">
  > *Turn this into a spreadsheet with columns: Task, Owner, Priority, Due date. [paste list]*
</Step>
<Step title="Check the columns">
  Preview the sheet. Ask for fixes: *"Sort by Due date"* or *"Add a Status column with Not started / In progress / Done."*
</Step>
</Steps>

**Good spreadsheet asks**
- *"Build a content calendar sheet for April with Date, Channel, Topic, Owner."*
- *"Make a budget tracker with Category, Planned, Actual, and Variance."*
- *"Convert this messy list of leads into a clean sheet."*

## PDFs {#pdfs}

Use PDFs when you need something polished to send, print, or attach.

<Steps>
<Step title="Say you want a PDF">
  > *Create a one-page PDF handout of our office welcome guide, clear and friendly.*
</Step>
<Step title="Add source material if you have it">
  Paste text, or point the Agent at [knowledge](/user-guide/knowledge) you've uploaded.
</Step>
<Step title="Download and share">
  Preview the PDF, download it, then send it or store it in [Storage](/user-guide/workspace-tour) if you use files there.
</Step>
</Steps>

**Good PDF asks**
- *"Make a PDF one-pager for our workshop agenda."*
- *"Turn this policy into a short PDF customers can read."*
- *"Create a PDF checklist for event day setup."*

## Tips that always help

| Do this | Why it helps |
|---|---|
| Name the **format** (doc / sheet / PDF) | The Agent aims at the right deliverable |
| Name the **audience** | Tone and detail stay appropriate |
| List **sections or columns** | Structure appears without extra rounds |
| Paste **your real content** | Less guessing, more accuracy |
| Ask for **one change at a time** | Easier to steer refinements |
